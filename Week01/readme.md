# Week 01

# UK Job & Labour Demand Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes **job and labour demand in the United Kingdom** over the **last 2–3 years**. The goal is to understand how demand for different occupations has changed over time, identify emerging trends, and extract meaningful insights about the UK labour market.

The analysis combines multiple official UK data sources to provide a comprehensive and reliable view of labour demand across sectors and occupations.

---

## Data Description

The dataset represents **labour demand and occupations in demand in the UK**, focusing on recent years to capture short‑term and medium‑term market trends.

The data highlights:

* Demand volumes by occupation
* Occupations currently in demand
* Labour market signals from employment and education statistics

---

## Data Sources

The data used in this project was gathered from the following official sources:

1. **Office for National Statistics (ONS)**
   Labour demand volumes by Standard Occupational Classification (SOC 2020):
   [https://www.ons.gov.uk/employmentandlabourmarket/peopleinwork/employmentandemployeetypes/datasets/labourdemandvolumesbystandardoccupationclassificationsoc2020uk](https://www.ons.gov.uk/employmentandlabourmarket/peopleinwork/employmentandemployeetypes/datasets/labourdemandvolumesbystandardoccupationclassificationsoc2020uk)

2. **NOMIS – Official Labour Market Statistics**
   UK labour market and employment dataset:
   [https://www.nomisweb.co.uk/query/construct/summary.asp?mode=construct&version=0&dataset=218](https://www.nomisweb.co.uk/query/construct/summary.asp?mode=construct&version=0&dataset=218)

3. **Explore Education Statistics (UK Government)**
   Occupations in demand (2025):
   [https://explore-education-statistics.service.gov.uk/find-statistics/occupations-in-demand/2025](https://explore-education-statistics.service.gov.uk/find-statistics/occupations-in-demand/2025)

## Tasks Performed

The following steps were carried out in this project:

1. **Loading and Understanding the Data**

   * Imported datasets from multiple sources
   * Reviewed structure, variables, and time coverage

2. **Data Cleaning**

   * Handled missing and inconsistent values
   * Standardized occupation names and classifications
   * Filtered data for relevant years

3. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**

   * Analyzed demand distribution across occupations
   * Compared trends across different time periods
   * Visualized key patterns using charts and graphs

4. **Estimating and Plotting Trends**

   * Identified growth and decline in job demand
   * Plotted time‑series trends for major occupations

5. **Gathering Insights**

   * Highlighted high‑growth and declining occupations
   * Interpreted labour market shifts
   * Derived insights relevant to policy, education, and workforce planning
